Abstract

Challenges

The increasing global demand for energy, coupled with the rapid depletion of fossil fuel resources, has emphasized the urgent need for clean, renewable, and sustainable energy alternatives. This challenge is reflected in Egyptâ€™s Grand Challenges, which include improving the use of alternative energy sources, reducing environmental pollution, and adapting to the effects of climate change.

Solution Idea

To address these pressing concerns, our project presents an innovative solution: a Magno Vertical Axis Wind Turbine (VAWT) designed to generate electricity using wind power while minimizing mechanical friction. The magno system reduces energy loss by suspending the turbine using a magnetic system, which eliminates contact-based friction, thereby enhancing efficiency and extending the lifespan of the system. The turbine is designed to operate efficiently in low wind conditions, making it suitable for urban and rural environments.

Design Properties

The design focuses on simplicity and practicality, making it cost-effective, scalable, and durable. This ensures the system can help reduce reliance on fossil fuels, contribute to climate change mitigation, and support long-term environmental sustainability.

Methods

Assembly Overview

This section explains the step-by-step construction process of the wind turbine prototype, including the assembly of the turbine, magnetic generator system, and supporting frame.

Turbine Assembly

The turbine blades are installed at the upper part of the prototype by attaching curved blades to a circular plate, which is then connected to the central vertical shaft. The blades are evenly spaced and balanced to ensure efficient wind capture.

Magnetic Generator System

The magnetic section is built in the middle by fixing permanent magnets onto a rotating disk connected to the shaft, while copper wire coils are placed on a nearby stationary plate. A small gap is maintained between the magnets and coils to allow effective electromagnetic induction without contact.

Generator Installation

The generator is mounted at the bottom of the structure by attaching a wasted motor or custom generator directly to the shaft. It is secured firmly to the bottom plate and carefully aligned to ensure smooth rotation.

Frame Assembly

The structural frame is assembled using columns that connect the top, middle, and bottom plates. Threaded rods or strong supports are used and tightly fastened with nuts and washers to maintain stability.

Base Platform

All parts are attached to the base platform, which is made heavy and wide enough to support the entire structure. The generator and columns are fixed to the base.

Project Idea

According to a 2015 study by Khammas et al., conventional power generation is expected to decrease due to environmental concerns, making wind energy a valuable alternative. Wind energy is abundant, low-cost, simple, scalable, eco-friendly, and allows control over output due to its variable speed. There are two main types of wind turbines: Vertical Axis Wind Turbines (VAWTs) and Horizontal Axis Wind Turbines (HAWTs). HAWTs are commonly used in large-scale farms, but VAWTs are smaller, more efficient, and do not need to face the wind. They can generate power directly and are increasingly supported by new technology. VAWTs are mainly categorized into Savonius and Darrieus types. The Savonius turbine is more practical in the low wind due to its simplicity and self-starting ability, with efficiency up to 37%. Darrieus turbines have lower efficiency (below 10%) and low starting torture, which reduces their reliability. A notable example of Savonius turbines is in Akihabara, Tokyo (Fig. 2). The proposed design is a Savonius VAWT with a dual electromagnetic generation system that converts rotor motion into electricity using magnets and copper coils. An Arduino-based monitoring system with sensors and screens tracks output. The generated electricity is stored in a lithium-ion battery. Since the generators produce AC while the battery needs DC, a bridge rectifier is used to convert AC to DC. The prototype uses a combination of recycled, 3D-printed, and electronic components, supporting sustainability and cost-efficiency. It meets design requirements: compact and user-friendly, with real-time monitoring, environmentally friendly through using wind energy and recycled materials (e.g., copper wire, bicycle spokes, microwave motor), fast energy production due to dual-generation, safe and space-efficient, and low-cost and scalable using open-source components like Arduin.

Arduino System

The system is designed to collect real-time data using two high-precision sensors. The first is the ZMPT101B, a voltage transformer module known for its compact size and exceptional accuracy in AC voltage measurement. It operates based on a precision voltage transformer and conditioning circuit, making it ideal for non-intrusive voltage monitoring in low-power applications. The second sensor is the ACS712, a fully integrated, Hall-effect-based linear current sensor. This sensor offers a broad current measurement range, typically Â±5A, Â±20A, or Â±30A depending on the model, and is capable of accurately detecting both AC and DC currents. It features low noise output, fast response time, and high linearity, making it suitable for precise current sensing in real-time power monitoring systems. Both sensors are configured to provide continuous feedback on the performance of the upper and lower generation units of the wind turbine system. These inputs are processed through an Arduino Uno microcontroller, which serves as the core of the data acquisition and control unit. The Arduino not only interprets analog signals from both sensors using its internal ADC (Analog-to-Digital Converter) but also controls a 16x2 LCD display for real-time visualization of voltage and current readings The integration of these components enables precise monitoring, control, and analysis of power generation performance. Negative & positive restults

Positive Results: The lower industrial wasted generator produced a high voltage output, reaching a maximum of 183 V. The upper handmade (DIY) generator, delivered a significantly high current peaking at 120.4 mA. The maximum power output recorded was 1.159 W. The total energy output over five minutes was 327.54 joules, shows the successful of the dual generation mechanism. The use of Arduino monitoring systems ensured accuracy and real-time measurements. AC electric current can be utilized to operate AC circuit components like Light Bulb. Despite the success in achieving the core product, the project faced certain issues that should be addressed in future updates of the design. Negative Results: The current output of the lower generator was relatively low (maximum 5.2 mA). The voltage output of the upper generator was insufficient (maximum 1.934 V). There was difficulty in synchronizing the outputs of the two systems due to different characteristics. Mechanical limitations in the prototype's structure, such as slight vibrations or instability at higher wind speeds, affected consistency. Electric current is an AC current which make a problem when combining the output together. Although the negative results are not easily resolved, they can be addressed through advanced engineering approaches and further research.
